      YEAR 30 · BREAKING

      The machines learned to think.

      General-purpose AI sweeps the global economy. Routine factory work, back-office services, call centres, bookkeeping — automated within years, not decades. Rich-country firms reshore production to robot-run plants. The ladder that every successful developer climbed for sixty years — cheap labour, light manufacturing, exports — has had its lowest rungs sawn off.

      Nobody told you this was coming. You planned for the world of Year 0. The question now: did your thirty years of choices leave your country ready — by accident or by wisdom?

      Instructor notes · model logic

      Growth each decade = base + convergence (catch-up) term + investment contributions, scaled by institutional quality, minus inequality drag. Key mechanics: diminishing returns (√points), complementarities (manufacturing needs infrastructure × institutions; R&D needs literacy × tertiary education), country multipliers (each archetype rewards different sectors), Dutch disease (resource reliance suppresses manufacturing unless offset), and path dependence (each decade's stocks become the next decade's starting conditions). AI-readiness is computed from tertiary education, digital infrastructure, institutions, R&D stock and infrastructure — minus an exposure penalty if the economy specialised in routine manufacturing or routine services with a low-skill workforce. Strategies that were "optimal" pre-shock can be punished by it: that is the point. A legitimacy stat (public approval) responds to growth, inequality, social spending and events; reforms can trigger elite backlash, and sustained low legitimacy or extreme inequality ends the game early in a revolution or coup — the political-economy constraint on technocratic planning.
